The Threat

How a Phishing Attack Unfolds

Phishing is the #1 entry point for data breaches. Understanding the playbook is the first step to defeating it.

1
The Bait

You receive an email, text, or social media message that looks official — from your bank, a delivery company, or even a colleague. It creates urgency: "Your account will be locked", "Verify now", "Unusual sign-in detected."

2
The Hook

The message contains a link. The URL looks plausible — perhaps secure-bank-login.com or paypa1.com. The landing page is a pixel-perfect copy of the real site, built in minutes using freely available tools.

3
The Catch

You enter your username and password. The attacker receives them instantly. Within minutes they log in to your real account, transfer funds, steal data, or use your identity to attack others.

Know the Tricks

Common Phishing Tactics

Attackers use a predictable toolkit. Once you know what to look for, the tricks become obvious.

Start learning
Lookalike domains
Replacing letters with similar characters — rn looks like m, 0 like O. Example: arnazon.com, paypa1.com.
Artificial urgency
"Your account will be suspended in 24 hours." Fear and time pressure make people skip their usual checks.
Fake trust signals
Padlock icons, "SSL Secured", and official-looking logos are images — they prove nothing about the actual site's authenticity.
Impersonation
Attackers forge the sender name to show a trusted brand. Always check the actual email address, not just the display name.
URL shorteners
Services like bit.ly hide the real destination. Hover over or expand shortened links before clicking.
