How to Protect Yourself
Always check the URL — read the full domain before typing anything. Look for misspellings or extra subdomains.
Use a password manager — it auto-fills only on the real domain, so it silently refuses phishing sites.
Enable two-factor authentication — even if your password is stolen, attackers can't access your account without the second factor.
Don't trust urgency — pressure to act immediately is the attacker's tool. Slow down and verify independently.
Navigate directly — type the address yourself or use a bookmark instead of clicking links from emails or messages.
Scan suspicious links first — use the PhishGuard URL Scanner before clicking any link you are uncertain about.
If This Were Real — Act Now
  1. Change your password on the real site immediately.
  2. Change the same password everywhere else you used it.
  3. Enable 2FA if you haven't already.
  4. Check for unauthorised activity and report it to the platform.
  5. Report the phishing URL to your browser and email provider.
